David S. Tourigny

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow of the Simons Foundation and LRSF hosted by Columbia University Medical Center, New York. Previously, I was a Research Fellow in the Department of Applied Mathematics and Theoretical Physics (DAMTP) at the University of Cambridge, where I also completed my PhD under the supervision of Venki Ramakrishnan and Garib Murshudov at the MRC Laboratory of Molecular Biology as a member of Trinity College.

Research

Spatiotemporal regulation of metabolism and protein synthesis

My current research focus is on understanding the dynamic regulation of metabolism in space and time. I have used both experimental and theoretical approaches to study metabolism in a variety of contexts, ranging from microbial systems to neuronal networks. Recently, I have become interested in the way that individual organisms and populations employ 'bet-hedging' strategies for optimal allocation of limited metabolic resources in the face of uncertainty (see Publications for more details). Having previously worked on the ribosome during my PhD, I have a longer-term ambition to study the integration of metabolism with protein synthesis as one of the most energy-demanding processes inside the cell.

Dynamical systems and control theory

Most of my work involves mathematical modelling based on Applied Dynamical Systems Theory and Optimal Control Theory. In the past, I also have worked on these topics in their own right with relatively less application. Specifically, this involved some geometric properties of certain dynamical systems (see Publications).
